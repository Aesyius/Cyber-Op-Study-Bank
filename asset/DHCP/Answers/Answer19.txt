DHCP troubleshooting techniques for resolving connectivity issues may include: ●Checking DHCP server availability and configuration settings. ●Verifying DHCP relay agent configurations for multi-subnet environments. ●Examining DHCP logs for error messages or lease allocation issues. ●Resolving network connectivity issues such as DNS resolution, routing, or firewall restrictions. ●Performing client-side troubleshooting, such as releasing and renewing DHCP leases or checking network adapter settings.
