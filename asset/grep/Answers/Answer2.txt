The syntax of the "grep" command is: grep [OPTIONS] PATTERN [FILE...] Commonly used options with the "grep" command include: ●-i: Ignore case distinctions during search. ●-r: Search recursively through directories. ●-v: Invert the match to display lines that do not match the pattern. ●-l: Display only the filenames that contain the pattern. ●-n: Display line numbers along with matching lines. ●-c: Count occurrences of matched patterns. ●-E: Enable extended regex syntax. ●-w: Match whole words only. ●-A: Display lines after the matching line. ●-B: Display lines before the matching line. ●-C: Display lines before and after the matching line.
