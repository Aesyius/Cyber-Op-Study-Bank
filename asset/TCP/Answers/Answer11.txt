Common TCP flags used in the TCP header include: ●SYN (Synchronize): Used to initiate a connection. ●ACK (Acknowledge): Confirms receipt of data or acknowledges sequence numbers. ●FIN (Finish): Signals the end of data transmission or connection termination. ●RST (Reset): Resets a connection or indicates an error. ●PSH (Push): Urges immediate data delivery without waiting for a full buffer. ●URG (Urgent): Indicates urgent data requiring immediate attention.
