The main fields in an IPv4 header and their information are as follows: ●Version: Specifies the IP version (IPv4 or IPv6). ●Header Length: Indicates the length of the IP header in 32-bit words. ●Type of Service (TOS): Specifies the quality of service for the datagram. ●Total Length: Indicates the total length of the datagram (header + payload). ●Identification, Flags, and Fragment Offset: Used for fragmentation and reassembly of datagrams. ●Time to Live (TTL): Specifies the maximum number of hops a datagram can traverse. ●Protocol: Identifies the upper-layer protocol (TCP, UDP, ICMP, etc.) used in the payload. ●Header Checksum: Ensures the integrity of the header during transmission. ●Source and Destination IP Addresses: Specify the source and destination devices' IP addresses.
